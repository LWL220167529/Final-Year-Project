Day 1

  • Sensō-ji
  • Tokyo National Museum
  • Ueno Zoo
  • Ueno Park

Morning

Begin your day early at Sensō-ji, Tokyo's oldest and most significant temple. Walk through the Kaminarimon (Thunder Gate), notable for its massive red lantern, and down Nakamise Street, a bustling market with traditional snacks and souvenirs. Visit the temple's main hall and the five-story pagoda. For breakfast, try some fresh 'ningyoyaki' (small cakes filled with red bean paste) from a street vendor.

Midday

Take a 30-minute leisurely walk or a short ride on the Tokyo Metro to Ueno, where the Tokyo National Museum is located. Immerse yourself in Japan's history and art, featuring samurai armor, antique pottery, and delicate scrolls. Have lunch at the museum's restaurant, where you can enjoy traditional Japanese cuisine with a modern twist.

Afternoon

After lunch, head to Ueno Zoo, a short walk within Ueno Park. Meet the giant pandas, a highlight of the zoo, and explore the diverse range of animal exhibits. Enjoy a matcha ice cream while strolling around the zoo.

Evening

Spend the remainder of the afternoon in Ueno Park, a spacious public park that's also home to multiple museums and a picturesque pond. Visit the Shinobazu Pond, especially beautiful during lotus blooming season. For dinner, explore the Ameyoko Market area near Ueno Park, offering a variety of street food and casual eateries where you can sample sushi, yakitori, and more. Return to your accommodation to rest for the night.

Day 2

  • Tokyo DisneySea
  • Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden
  • Meiji Jingu
  • Zōjō-ji Temple

Morning

Start your day at Tokyo DisneySea, the only Disney park of its kind in the world. Enjoy themed areas like Mediterranean Harbor and Mysterious Island. Don't miss the unique rides such as "Journey to the Center of the Earth" and "20,000 Leagues Under the Sea." Have a sea-themed breakfast at one of the park's many restaurants, like the New York Deli for a quick bagel or sandwich.

Midday

Leave DisneySea after the morning and travel to Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden. Relax in one of Tokyo's largest and most popular gardens, which features three distinct styles: traditional Japanese, formal French, and an English landscape. Enjoy a bento box under the cherry blossoms (if in season) or visit the teahouse for a green tea experience.

Afternoon

After a peaceful lunch, make your way to Meiji Shrine, dedicated to Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken. Cleanse your hands and mouth at the temizuya (water ablution basin) before entering. Take a moment to write a wish on an ema (wooden plaque).

Evening

Your next stop is Zōjō-ji Temple, an important temple of the Jodo sect of Japanese Buddhism. Observe the 'Unborn Children Garden' with rows of stone statues representing unborn children. Have dinner at one of the restaurants in Roppongi, which is close to Zōjō-ji. Try some kaiseki (traditional multi-course Japanese dinner) to end your day.

Day 3

  • Tokyo Disneyland
  • Tokyo Tower
  • Imperial Palace
  • Kiyosumi Gardens

Morning

Embark on a magical morning at Tokyo Disneyland, where fairy tales come to life. Experience classic attractions like "It's a Small World" and "Pirates of the Caribbean." Grab a Mickey-shaped snack as you enjoy the various parades and shows.

Midday

Head to Tokyo Tower around noon. Opt for a bento from a nearby convenience store to enjoy in the adjacent Shiba Park. Ascend the tower for panoramic views of the city.

Afternoon

A short metro ride will take you to the Imperial Palace. Visit the East Gardens, which are part of the inner palace area and are open to the public. Walk along the moats and massive stone walls while learning about the history of the Edo Castle.

Evening

Conclude your sightseeing at Kiyosumi Gardens, a traditional stroll garden. Enjoy the serene setting with stepping stones, a large pond, and beautifully landscaped paths. Try some dorayaki (red bean pancake) from a nearby shop. For your final night, choose a dinner venue in the bustling district of Shibuya. Indulge in a hearty bowl of ramen or visit an izakaya (Japanese pub) for small plates and drinks. Reflect on your adventure as you prepare for your departure or the continuation of your travels.
